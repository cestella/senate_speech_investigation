Washington – U.S. Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor today urged their colleagues to support the nomination of Mr. William Conner Eldridge, Jr. of Arkadelphia to serve as the U.S. Attorney for the Western District of Arkansas. The Senate Judiciary Committee approved Mr. Eldridge’s nomination today, and he must now be confirmed by the full Senate.
Senators Lincoln and Pryor recommended Mr. Eldridge for this position in May 2009. He is currently the Chief Executive Officer of Summit Bank.
“I am pleased that the Senate Judiciary Committee has moved forward with Conner’s nomination today, and I am hopeful he will be confirmed by the entire Senate quickly,” Lincoln said. “I have known Conner for years, and I was proud to recommend him for the position of U.S. Attorney for the Western District. I believe he has the experience, intellect and integrity to serve the people of Arkansas with distinction.”
“Conner Eldridge’s strong legal experience as well as his time in the private sector make him well-qualified to serve as U.S. Attorney for Arkansas’s Western District,” Pryor said.  “I am pleased that the Senate Judiciary Committee has approved Mr. Eldridge’s nomination, and I look forward to a speedy confirmation by the full Senate.”  
Conner Eldridge has served as a Special Deputy Prosecutor for the Prosecuting Attorney’s Office of Clark County, Arkansas since 2009.  Eldridge previously worked for Summit Bank and Summit Bancorp, Inc., from 2004 to 2010, serving in various senior management positions.  He was named Chief Executive Officer in 2008.  Following law school, he was a law clerk for the Honorable G. Thomas Eisele of the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Arkansas from 2003 to 2004.  Eldridge graduated from Davidson College in 1999 and from the University of Arkansas School of Law in 2003.
