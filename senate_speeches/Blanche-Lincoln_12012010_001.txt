Washington – U.S. Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor are proud to announce that Christopher R. Thyer of Jonesboro has been nominated by President Obama for the position of United States Attorney for the Eastern District of Arkansas.
Senators Lincoln and Pryor recommended Mr. Thyer for this position in May 2009.
“I am proud to have recommended Mr. Thyer for the position of U.S. Attorney for the Eastern District and I am pleased that the White House has moved forward with his nomination,” Lincoln said. “Mr. Thyer has considerable experience practicing law in Arkansas along with a distinguished record of service in the state House of Representatives. I have confidence in his ability to serve our state with distinction and I hope that his nomination will proceed quickly.”
“Mr. Thyer’s legal experience in Arkansas as well as his time in the Arkansas House of Representatives make him a strong candidate for United States Attorney for our Eastern District,” Pryor said.  “I look forward to introducing Mr. Thyer to my Senate colleagues and hope that his confirmation process proceeds swiftly and efficiently.  I am confident he will serve Arkansas’s Eastern District well.”
Christopher R. Thyer has been a partner at Stanley & Thyer, P.A., since 2007.  Prior to this, Thyer was a partner at Halsey & Thyer, PLC from 2005 to 2007, and at Mooney Law Firm, P.A., from 1997 to 2005.  From 1995 to 1997, he was a sole practitioner.  He also served in the Arkansas House of Representatives as a State Representative for the 74th District from 2003 to 2009.  Thyer graduated from Arkansas State University in 1991 and from the University of Arkansas School of Law in 1995.
