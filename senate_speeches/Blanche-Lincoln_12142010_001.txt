Washington – U.S. Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor today applauded the confirmation of Mr. William Conner Eldridge, Jr. of Arkadelphia to serve as the U.S. Attorney for the Western District of Arkansas. The Senate recently confirmed his nomination.
Senators Lincoln and Pryor recommended Mr. Eldridge for this position in May 2009. He is currently the Chief Executive Officer of Summit Bank.
“I am pleased that the Senate has confirmed Conner’s nomination,” Lincoln said. “I have known Conner for years, and I was proud to recommend him for the position of U.S. Attorney for the Western District. I believe he has the experience, intellect and integrity to serve the people of Arkansas with distinction.”
“Conner Eldridge has experience in both the legal and private sectors that makes him a good fit for U.S. Attorney for the Western District of Arkansas,” Pryor said.  “I am pleased that the Senate has confirmed Mr. Eldridge’s nomination, and I am confident that he will serve Arkansas’s Western District well.”
Conner Eldridge has served as a Special Deputy Prosecutor for the Prosecuting Attorney’s Office of Clark County, Arkansas since 2009.  Eldridge previously worked for Summit Bank and Summit Bancorp, Inc., from 2004 to 2010, serving in various senior management positions.  He was named Chief Executive Officer in 2008.  Following law school, he was a law clerk for the Honorable G. Thomas Eisele of the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Arkansas from 2003 to 2004.  Eldridge graduated from Davidson College in 1999 and from the University of Arkansas School of Law in 2003.
