U.S. Sen. Jim Inhofe (R-Okla.), a senior member of the Senate Armed Services Committee (SASC), today made the following statement after the United States Army appointed Bill Burgess as a Civilian Aide to the Secretary of the Army:

“Today, I congratulate Bill Burgess on his appointment as Civilian Aide to the Secretary of the Army. As a colleague of mine, I admire Bill’s leadership and long-term advocacy for the state of Oklahoma. Now, as he begins serving as a Civilian Aide to the United States Army, I look forward to working with him to advance the needs of the Secretary and the United States Army.” 

About Bill

A recipient of awards including the “I Believe in Oklahoma” campaign and Entrepreneur of the Year, Bill Burgess has served Oklahoma and the nation as a tireless advocate to advancing education and serving the youth. Additionally, Burgess has served as the former president of the Boy Scouts of America-Oklahoma council and was one of the youngest individuals ever named to serve as a member of the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education.
